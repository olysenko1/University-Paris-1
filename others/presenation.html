What is a Sovereign Debt Crisis

Sovereign debt crisis occurs when country experiences difficulty in funding its debt, which is visible in the increasing interest rates. (Ureche-Rangau - Burietz, 2013)

Events occurring when either a country defaults or its bond spreads are above a critical threshold. (Pescatori - Sy, 2007)

2008 Financial Crisis

Period of severe financial distress and drying up of liquidity, which originated from U.S subprime mortgage market and soon spread to the global financial system through the contagion and financial interconnectedness.

The GIIPS countries

Greece: public debt (Bailout: May, 2010)

Italy: public and private debt

Ireland: private debt transmitted to public debt (Bailout: November, 2010)

Portugal: public debt (Bailout: May, 2011)

Spain: private debt transmitted to public debt (Bailout: May, 2010)

European Debt crisis

  • Late 2009 - GIIPS countries report on huge deficit/GDP ratios.
  • Greece announce budget deficit of 12.7 %.
  • Drastic interest rate increase on Greek bonds.
  • Burst of the bubble in real estate market in Ireland and Spain, consequent banking crisis and bailout by government. The private debt mutated into public debt. (Acharya et al, 2011)

Euro crisis as a combination of three crises (Shambaugh et al, 2012)

Causes of the European crisis

1.Long-term causes within GIIPS

  • Current account imbalances within the EU members
  • Uniform monetary policy across heterogeneous countries
  • Moral hazard problems

2.Long-term causes within GIIPS

  • Credit boom due to low interest rates after joining the Euro
  • Miss-allocation of investments
  • Failure to tighten the fiscal policy
  • Disregard for pro-cyclicality of the budget balance

3.Financial crisis

  • Unprecedented scale
  • High contagion
  • Increased risk-aversion
  • Transfer to sovereign crisis
  • Fiscal burden of the crisis and consequent recession

of factors.
